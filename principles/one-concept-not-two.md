# One Concept, Not Two

ODPM has one primary concept: Ontology.

Not Understanding. Not Shared Understanding. Ontology.

## Why Not Two Terms

Every time ODPM introduced two terms for nearly the same idea, confusion followed.

Understanding. Shared Understanding. Ontology.

People asked "what's the difference?" instead of learning ODPM.

## Shared Is a Property, Not a Noun

"Shared" describes a quality the ontology must have. It is not a separate thing to build.

Instead of: Build shared understanding.
Say: Build a shared ontology.

Instead of: We don't have shared understanding yet.
Say: Our ontology isn't aligned yet.

## What This Avoids

ODPM does not need to settle whether understanding lives in people's minds, in conversation, or in documentation.

ODPM only needs to define the object teams collaborate around.

> Ontology is the team's shared model of the world.

Whether that model also exists mentally or socially is a separate question — one ODPM does not need to answer.

## Understanding Is Still a Practice

Removing "Shared Understanding" as a noun does not remove understanding.

Understanding remains a human activity — what Chase Understanding describes, what conversation builds.

Ontology is the shared object that activity produces and that ODPM manages.

## The Simplified Lifecycle

Conversation builds the ontology.

Artifacts express it.

Execution tests it.

Feedback refines it.
