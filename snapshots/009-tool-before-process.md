# Snapshot 009: Tool Before Process

Date: 2026-07-01

ODPM has always described itself as a way of thinking.

This is a note about the moment it stopped being only a way of thinking and became an instrument — and about the one step still missing before it becomes a standing process.

---

## The Distinction

A tool is something you pick up, use, and get a result from.

A process is something that keeps working after you put it down.

ODPM is now proven as a tool. Run the ontology lens over an existing piece of work and it produces real findings.

It is not yet proven as a process. The findings it produces have nowhere to live between runs.

---

## Why It Became Practical

Three things moved ODPM from plausible to practical.

**It attached to an artifact that already existed.**

ODPM did not ask anyone to produce a new model from scratch.

It gave a reading lens for structure that was already present in the work.

That is the difference between a framework you adopt — cost first, benefit later, usually abandoned — and one that describes what you already have — benefit first.

Practicality came from zero new-artifact cost, not from discipline.

**It produced claims that could be wrong.**

An idea stays theoretical as long as its outputs cannot be falsified.

The moment the ontology made a specific, checkable claim — this many states reduce to that many, this path is never reached, this concept is not modeled where it should be — it stopped being philosophy and became a statement reality could contradict.

Some of those claims were wrong, and were corrected on the record.

An idea that can be wrong on the record is a practical idea.

**Its value showed up as findings, not documents.**

The document is the byproduct.

The real output was a small set of specific, verifiable problems and one reusable concept.

A framework that only returns "better shared understanding" never clears the bar of practicality.

This one cleared it because the understanding cashed out as named problems someone could act on.

---

## The Transferable Pattern

The mechanism is not specific to ODPM. It transfers to any framework at risk of staying a "nice idea."

> Bind the abstract framework to an artifact the team already maintains. Force it to emit claims the artifact can confirm or refute. Measure it by the problems it finds, not the documents it produces.

ODPM supplied the vocabulary: Concept, Relationship, Rule, State, Action.

The work being examined supplied the instances.

None of the value lived in the framework alone or in the work alone.

It lived in the gap between what the intent said, what the ontology implied, and what the work actually did.

That gap is where every finding came from.

---

## The Missing Step

The tool works per-run. The process does not yet hold between runs.

The gap is specific: a finding has no home.

A discovered problem sits in a list of open questions until someone happens to read it. It is not tracked, not owned, not staged for a decision. The method is practical each time it is picked up, and leaks its output every time it is put down.

The difference between a tool and a process is one wiring step:

> question → tracked item → owner

---

## The Framework Already Named Its Own Fix

ODPM's own repository already specifies the missing mechanism.

`github-as-odpm-tool.md` maps an Issue to an Open Question, a Label to the epistemic status of that question, and a Merge to agreement.

That is exactly the wiring the discovery instrument lacks.

The open questions a review produces are meant to become Issues.

Their status is meant to become Labels.

Their resolution is meant to become agreement.

The framework predicted its own missing piece.

It has not yet been installed.

---

## The Open Question

> What is the smallest change that turns a per-run discovery instrument into a standing process — and is it purely mechanical (every open question becomes a tracked Issue), or does something about ownership and agreement have to change with it?
