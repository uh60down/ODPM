# Snapshot 002: PRD Ontology

Today's snapshot defines the first ODPM ontology for PRDs.

A PRD should be understood as a structured decision record, not merely a requirements document. Its value comes from showing how a team moved from uncertainty to commitment.

## Core Objects

- Context: the situation that makes the work relevant.
- Customer: the person, role, or system affected.
- Problem: the gap between the current state and desired state.
- Evidence: observations that support or challenge the framing.
- Requirement: a condition the solution must satisfy.
- Decision: a choice made by the team.
- Alternative: a plausible path not chosen.
- Constraint: a boundary that limits available choices.
- Risk: a possible future failure mode.
- Commitment: an action the organization is now taking.
- Learning: what must be checked after delivery.

## Relationships

- Context frames the problem.
- Evidence supports or weakens the problem.
- Requirements express what a good solution must satisfy.
- Decisions select among alternatives.
- Constraints shape decisions.
- Risks attach to assumptions, decisions, and commitments.
- Learning closes the loop after release.

## Working Standard

A PRD is ready when a reader can tell what decision is being made, why it matters, what evidence supports it, what tradeoffs were accepted, and what the team will learn next.
